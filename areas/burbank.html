Architecture & Roofing in Burbank

Burbank's building stock tells the story of a working city that grew alongside the entertainment industry. The Magnolia Park neighborhood features charming 1920s and 1930s Craftsman bungalows with steeply pitched roofs originally clad in wood shingle or composition. The flatlands south of Glenoaks Boulevard are lined with 1950s ranch homes carrying low-slope composition shingle roofs, many of which have been re-roofed at least once. Up in the Rancho Equestrian district and the Verdugo foothills, hillside contemporaries sport a mix of tile, metal, and flat roof systems adapted to steep terrain. Near the studios and along the commercial corridors of Olive Avenue and San Fernando Boulevard, single-ply membrane roofs and built-up systems cover the city's substantial inventory of commercial and light industrial buildings.

Common Roofing Challenges in Burbank

Burbank sits squarely in the San Fernando Valley heat zone, where summer temperatures routinely exceed 100 degrees and subject roofing materials to intense UV degradation and thermal stress. The city's post-war housing boom means thousands of homes carry roofs that are 30 to 50 years old and approaching the end of their serviceable life. Properties in the Verdugo foothills present access challenges for materials delivery and crew staging, with steep driveways and narrow hillside roads that require careful logistics planning. On the commercial side, the large flat-roofed buildings near the studios and the Burbank Airport corridor need TPO or PVC membrane systems designed for ponding water management and energy efficiency. Burbank's position at the base of the Verdugo Mountains also places hillside homes in a fire hazard zone, adding fire-rated material requirements to the equation.

Our Burbank Roofing Services

Shingle Roofing

The workhorse of Burbank residential roofing, architectural shingles provide the heat resistance and value that flatland ranch homes and Magnolia Park bungalows need. We install premium shingle lines with enhanced UV protection engineered for the San Fernando Valley's punishing summer conditions.

Flat Roof Systems

Burbank's commercial buildings, studios, and mid-century modern homes rely on flat and low-slope roof systems for weather protection. We install TPO and PVC single-ply membranes with proper drainage design, delivering energy-efficient roofing that handles the valley heat and occasional heavy rain events.

◊

Tile Roofing

Hillside homes in the Rancho Equestrian district and along the Verdugo foothills often feature tile roofs that demand specialized installation on steep slopes. Our crews have the experience and equipment to work safely on these elevated properties, installing fire-rated tile systems with proper battens and fastening for high-wind performance.
